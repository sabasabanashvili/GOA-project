Welcome to Georgia

Georgia is a country located at the crossroads of Europe and Asia, known for its diverse culture, stunning landscapes, and rich history. Nestled between the Black Sea to the west and the Caucasus Mountains to the north, it offers a unique blend of natural beauty and ancient traditions.

About Georgia

Georgia (საქართველო, Sakartvelo) is a country in the Caucasus region of Eurasia. It is bordered by Russia to the north, Turkey and Armenia to the south, and Azerbaijan to the southeast. The capital of Georgia is Tbilisi, a city with a rich cultural heritage that dates back to the 5th century. Georgia is famous for its ancient winemaking traditions, beautiful landscapes, and diverse architecture, ranging from medieval fortresses to modern buildings.

Did You Know? – Fascinating Facts About Georgia

Georgia's Ancient Winemaking Tradition

Georgia is one of the oldest wine-producing countries in the world. Archaeological evidence shows that wine production began in the region over 8,000 years ago. The unique climate and soil conditions in Georgia's vineyards have made the country famous for its wines. Some of the oldest and most unique grape varieties are cultivated here, making Georgian wine a rare and sought-after product. Wine is so important to the culture that it has even been designated as an intangible cultural heritage by UNESCO.

Georgian Language – One of the Oldest in the World

The Georgian language (ქართული) is one of the oldest languages in the world. It is part of the Kartvelian language family and has its own unique script, which dates back to the 5th century. The Georgian alphabet is unique and contains 33 letters, each representing a specific sound. The language is known for its complexity, with three different scripts: Asomtavruli, Nuskhuri, and Mkhedruli. Today, Georgian is spoken by around 4 million people, mostly in Georgia, and it is one of the official languages of the country.

The Rich Culture and Traditions of Georgia

Georgia has a rich and diverse cultural heritage influenced by its location at the crossroads of Europe and Asia. The country's cultural traditions, such as music, dance, and cuisine, have been shaped by a variety of cultures over the centuries. Georgian polyphonic singing is UNESCO-listed as a masterpiece of oral and intangible heritage. The famous Georgian dance, known for its elegance and energy, is performed at various festivals and events across the country.

Geography and Natural Wonders

Georgia is a land of breathtaking natural beauty. The country is home to diverse landscapes, ranging from the snowy peaks of the Caucasus Mountains in the north to the subtropical climate of the Black Sea coast. The country’s unique geography provides a habitat for rare species of plants and animals. Georgia’s national parks, such as the Kazbegi National Park and the Tusheti National Park, are perfect for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. The diverse climate and geography also make it an excellent destination for hiking, skiing, and other outdoor activities.

The Hospitality of the Georgian People

Georgians are known for their hospitality. The tradition of welcoming guests with open arms is deeply embedded in the culture. One of the most famous Georgian traditions is the “Supra” – a traditional feast where guests are invited to enjoy delicious food, drink, and live music. During a Supra, the host offers a toast (known as a "Tamada") and shares stories, poems, and songs. It’s an experience of warmth, friendship, and celebration that embodies the spirit of Georgian culture.
